Pete, Andy

- Missing the bigger picture
	- CBT is a tick box exercise, it represents the absolute minimum that's expected of companies
	- In the three weeks we spend onboarding new starters, cyber security isn't mentioned
	- The CBT is considered as a low value "must do" footnote

- Threat analysis
	- The volume of social engineering attacks is increasing at a parabolic rate
	- Historically you needed a good command of english to launch a voice based attack (AI removed this limitation)
	- One of the fasted increasing attack vectors is supply chain - that's us
	
-  Reviewed stats on phishing emails detected vs reported
	- Currently blocking over 99.99% - Significantly better than industry standards
	- This means that Cyber Security is a cultural challenge not a technology one
	- Disproportionately higher reports from UK than US (Same as data breaches)

 - We need to mature our approach to cyber security by making it part of our culture
	 - Cyber Security needs to be driven from the top down, not IT
	 - Embed security into existing rituals, a 2 minute touchpoint at the end of a team meeting is better than a mandatory hour long CBT session
	 - Security champions in each area, creating peer accountability
	 - Avoid a "gotcha" culture in favour of learning opportunities

- Action
	- Learning opportunity - Perform a cyber security risk assessment in AP (desktop exercise)

**Bloom’s Taxonomy** is a hierarchical framework for classifying educational goals, developed by Benjamin Bloom. It categorizes levels of cognitive learning from basic recall to complex critical thinking.

  

**Six Levels of Bloom’s Taxonomy (Revised Version)**

1. **Remembering** – Recall facts, terms, and basic concepts.

• Example: “List the steps of photosynthesis.”

1. **Understanding** – Explain ideas or concepts in your own words.

• Example: “Summarize the main idea of a novel.”

1. **Applying** – Use knowledge in new situations.

• Example: “Solve a math problem using a learned formula.”

1. **Analyzing** – Break down information and find relationships.

• Example: “Compare and contrast two political systems.”

1. **Evaluating** – Judge based on criteria, evidence, or logic.

• Example: “Assess the reliability of a news article.”

1. **Creating** – Produce new ideas, solutions, or products.

• Example: “Design an experiment to test a hypothesis.”

  

**Uses of Bloom’s Taxonomy**

• Helps design **effective learning objectives** and assessments.

• Encourages **higher-order thinking** beyond memorization.

• Useful for **teachers, trainers, and self-learners**.

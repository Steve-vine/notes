**Six Thinking Hats** is a decision-making and problem-solving framework developed by Edward de Bono. It helps individuals and groups think from multiple perspectives by assigning different “hats” (modes of thinking) to guide discussions systematically.

  

**The Six Hats & Their Roles**

1. **White Hat (Facts & Information)**

• Focuses on objective data, numbers, and available evidence.

• Example: “What do we know? What information is missing?”

1. **Red Hat (Emotions & Intuition)**

• Encourages expression of feelings, gut reactions, and instincts.

• Example: “How do we feel about this idea?”

1. **Black Hat (Critical Judgment & Risks)**

• Identifies potential problems, risks, and weaknesses.

• Example: “What could go wrong? What are the downsides?”

1. **Yellow Hat (Optimism & Benefits)**

• Highlights the positives, opportunities, and potential benefits.

• Example: “What are the advantages? Why is this a good idea?”

1. **Green Hat (Creativity & Innovation)**

• Encourages new ideas, alternatives, and creative solutions.

• Example: “Are there other ways to approach this?”

1. **Blue Hat (Process & Control)**

• Manages the thinking process, keeps discussions on track.

• Example: “What is the objective? What should we do next?”

  

**How It’s Used**

• Used in **meetings, brainstorming, and decision-making**.

• Helps avoid bias by ensuring all perspectives are considered.

• Can be applied **individually or in teams** to evaluate options.

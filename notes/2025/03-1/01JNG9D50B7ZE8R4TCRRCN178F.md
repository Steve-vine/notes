**Bayesian reasoning** is a logical approach to updating beliefs based on new evidence, using **Bayes’ Theorem**. It helps in making rational decisions by continuously adjusting probabilities as new data comes in.

  

**Key Principles**

1. **Prior Belief (Prior Probability)** – Your initial assumption about something before new evidence appears.

2. **New Evidence (Likelihood)** – The probability of observing the new evidence if your assumption is true.

3. **Updated Belief (Posterior Probability)** – A revised probability after considering new evidence.

**Real-World Examples**

• **Medical Diagnosis** – Doctors update disease likelihood based on test results.

• **Spam Filtering** – Email systems adjust spam detection based on patterns.

• **Legal Reasoning** – Juries weigh prior evidence with new witness testimony.

• **Decision Making** – Investors adjust risk assessments based on market trends.

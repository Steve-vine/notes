For large of complex requests, the company can extend the deadline up to 3 months.

If the request is unclear, or if the company is waiting for a response they can stop the clock.

### Can we charge a fee?
Not usually. In most cases you cannot charge a fee to comply with a SAR. However, you can charge a ’reasonable fee’ for the administrative costs of complying with a request if it is manifestly unfounded or excessive, or if an individual requests further copies of their data.

If the organisation charges a fee, the one month limit doesn't begin until that fee has been paid.

The right of access only applies to the individual’s personal data contained in the email. 

**Example**

An employee makes a SAR for all of the information you hold about them. During your search for their personal data, you find 2000 emails which the employee is copied into as a recipient. Other than their name and email address, the content of the emails does not relate to the employee or contain the employee’s personal data.

You do not have to provide the employee with a copy of each email (with the personal information of third parties redacted). Since the only personal data which relates to them is their name and email address, it is sufficient to advise them that you identified their name and email address on 2000 emails and disclose to them the name contained on those emails, eg John Smith, and the email address contained on those emails, eg JohnSmith@org.co.uk. Alternatively you could provide one email with other details redacted as a sample of the 2000 emails you hold. You should also clearly explain to the individual why this is the only information they are entitled to under the UK GDPR, but remember to provide them with supplementary information concerning the processing, eg retention periods for the emails.

When a Fee Can Be Charged:

- **Manifestly unfounded or excessive requests:**
    
    This applies when a request is clearly baseless or designed to cause disruption, not for genuine access to personal data. 
    
- **[Excessive](https://www.google.com/search?cs=1&sca_esv=5cbb6f2e087ee285&sxsrf=AE3TifOM1l7cFPA--n5VUJMvaIwd8ZpqCw%3A1755156291071&q=Excessive&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwjhqeTZ4omPAxVfT0EAHa4sNpkQxccNegQIERAB&mstk=AUtExfBdDVxEclXZmw4kHKu36mLUr6bq3rx6zqfNzkJLn4nGRyNfr4vKo0tKGxJx3xxy3zCrzCwA2KByyxoGuuSHOE6wC5wQw9zIfaER_9baVhUgFnTQ3qCEDf08m8eeTzPh0UD14wanqkLg4X5WzQJSgW6JHXG1SWXPoSbJkMYXlegz57k&csui=3) or repetitive requests:**
    
    If an individual repeatedly asks for the same information, especially shortly after receiving it, the organization can charge a fee. 
    
- **Requests for further copies:**
    
    If an individual asks for a copy of the same information previously provided, a reasonable fee may be applied.


What constitutes a "reasonable fee":

- The fee should be based on the actual administrative costs incurred by the organization in handling the request.

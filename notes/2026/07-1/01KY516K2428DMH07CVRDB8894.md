The third knowledge source made real (Canon: source 3, "Learned — incident experience"): working incidents teaches the graph.

- At incident resolution (the Update bookend, ADR 0029): the AI reviews the incident's evidence and diagnosis and proposes candidate `depends-on` edges ("the RDS outage took down Kora web UI → kora-web depends-on this database") into the **proposals queue**, citing the incident (IN-nnnn) as provenance.
- **Never auto-asserted**; one incident is weak evidence. A recurrence that would re-propose an existing pending proposal *strengthens* it (occurrence count on the proposal) rather than duplicating; a confirmed edge is not re-proposed; a rejected one is remembered.
- Attach to the most stable subject available: prefer service/workload-level entities over the transient node/pod the failure happened to sit on.
- Cost posture: this rides the existing resolution-time processing — no new scheduled AI work.
- Tests: proposal creation from a resolved-incident fixture; strengthen-not-duplicate; stable-subject selection.

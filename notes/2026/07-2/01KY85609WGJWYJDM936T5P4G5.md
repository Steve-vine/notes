Repro (Steve, 2026-07-23): Chinwag group has 14 members (ring cap 12). Rooted at Chinwag: 12 shown + "+2 more" — looks right, but only because the root happens to be the hidden nodes' real parent. Re-root at a member X (ISE-232): the other 13 members sit at depth 2 with parent **Chinwag**, ring shows 12 + "+1 more", and the placeholder renders as *X → "+1 more"* — claiming X points at something it has no relationship with. Expanding replaces it with the real 14th node whose edge is built from its true parent, so the phantom arrow vanishes and the correct Chinwag arrow appears.

Cause: `buildElements` (`graphLayout.ts`, the `hidden > 0` branch) hard-wires the placeholder's edge to `source: rootId`, regardless of where the hidden nodes actually hang. Since the edge is typed `'ise'`, it even gets the ISE-231 directional arrowhead, making the false claim look confident. `addEdge` directly above already knows the rule — "if the parent was filtered away, skip the edge rather than invent a spoke to the root (which would misstate topology)" — the "+N more" branch never got the same treatment.

Distinct from ISE-234 (arrow-flip misdirects a *real* edge, backend-led); this invents a *nonexistent* edge and is frontend-only.

Fix (self-contained in `buildElements`):
- The hidden nodes are known (`ordered.slice(RING_CAP)`): draw the placeholder's edge(s) from the **distinct real parents of the hidden nodes** (usually one; possibly several on a mixed ring) instead of the root.
- Style as a hint, not a claim: dashed, no arrowhead, no edge-type pill — it's an aggregate pointer, not a real relationship.
- Assert in the off-DOM `buildElements` tests (jsdom can't render @xyflow edges): re-rooted case draws the placeholder edge from the hidden nodes' parent, not the root; multi-parent ring draws one hint edge per distinct parent.

Done when: re-rooting at a Chinwag member never shows an arrow from that member to "+N more"; the placeholder hangs off Chinwag (the hidden members' parent) until expanded.

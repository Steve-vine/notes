Softer follow-up to DEV-995, worth having even once no-op saves are fixed (older clients, races with a real-but-tiny edit): when git-sync hits a conflicted note file, compare the two sides **ignoring** `updated:`. If one side's change is timestamp-only, there is no divergent content — keep the substantive side outright, no conflict copy, no keep-both.

This is exactly the ISE-73 case of 2026-07-18: the remote side added a comment, the local side was a pure `updated:` bump, and keep-both split the note anyway — stranding the comment on a copy the user then has to notice, mine, and delete.

DEV-995 removes the main source of timestamp-only sides; this makes the merge itself robust to any that still occur.
